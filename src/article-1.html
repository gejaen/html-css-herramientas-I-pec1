A Friends Pop-Up Is Coming to New York City

By
Joyce Chen

July 29, 2019

It’s been a whopping 25 years since Friends first premiered in September 1994, but fans of the hit series are no less fond of the six New York pals who helped define an era. And now, loyal Friends viewers will get the chance to revisit some of the show’s most iconic spaces with an interactive pop-up experience coming to New York City this fall.

In addition to the legendary Central Perk coffee shop (including its iconic orange couch, of course), the pop-up will feature walk-through replicas of Monica (Courteney Cox) and Rachel (Jennifer Aniston)’s apartment, Chandler (Matthew Perry) and Joey (Matt LeBlanc)’s apartment, and even the infamous Central Park fountain from the series’ opening credits, explained Warner Bros. in a press release. Visitors are advised to be Instagram-ready to snap photos of themselves with a turkey on their heads à la Joey in that one Thanksgiving episode, or carrying a sofa up a narrow flight of stairs like Ross (David Schwimmer) did on moving day. Other fun interactive elements include peeking through Rachel and Monica’s door into their purple apartment and kicking back in Joey and Chandlers’ recliners (chick and duck not included).

“It’s been nearly 25 years since Friends premiered, but the fascination and universal appeal of the hit TV series lives on with fans of all ages. As we celebrate the show’s milestone anniversary, we are excited to bring the Friends experience to life for our fans in a way that pays homage to the remarkable cast of characters, iconic sets and instantly quotable moments,” said Peter van Roden, SVP at Warner Bros. Consumer Products. Tickets for the pop-up, which will be located in Manhattan’s SoHo neighborhood, will go on sale Friday, August 2, and the special experience will run from September 7 through October 6.

For fans who might not be able to make it to the Big Apple, there are plenty of other ways to celebrate the show’s 25th anniversary, including a GIF channel that Warner Bros. is launching in partnership with Giphy, which will include a whopping 2,300 GIFs from every single episode of the series’ 10 seasons. Pottery Barn also launched a Friends-inspired furniture line earlier this month.
